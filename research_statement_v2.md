Postdoctoral Directions

I bring an integrated toolkit — DCE design, structural estimation, Bayesian simulation,
and causal inference — that can be directly applied to health decision-making,
digital health intervention design, and behavioral policy evaluation. My work bridges
behavioral economics and computational social science, producing both peer-reviewed
methods and deployable simulation tools.

I am seeking a postdoctoral position where I can extend this behavioral-AI research
agenda within a collaborative, interdisciplinary group. I aim to apply DCE-derived
behavioral models to clinical decision contexts and policy simulation, helping design
interventions that account for heterogeneous preferences, institutional trust, and
implementation constraints.
